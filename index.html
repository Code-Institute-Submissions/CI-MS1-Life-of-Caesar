The Life of Julius Caesar


By Gaius Suetonius Tranquillus


Published in 121 AD along with 11 other biographies collectively known as "The Twelve Caesars", this is an almost 2000 year old document reproduced online. Read about the famous Julius Caesar in the Romans' own words!

What is this site?


This is a reproduction of an important piece of history. On this site, you'll find reproduced the full text of a nearly 2000 year old biography of Julius Caesar, as well as further links to learn about the life of this incredibly fascinating historical figure!

This version was sourced from the university of Chicago which has generously put this public-domain translation online. By clicking the link, you can also view the original latin version as well as notations from scholars!

Why read about Caesar?


Because it's FUN! The main purpose of this page is to make history not just accesible to you, but also exciting and relatable. What I want you to take away from this site is not answers, but questions about a period two millenia in the past and yet so similar to our own!

If you find the text of Suetonius history hard to read, don't panic. This was written 2000 years ago and aimed for an audience that spoke a different language, from a culture that is incredibly different to our modern day sensibilities. Instead of focusing on what makes it different, focus on what you recognize as similar to our own time and you'll find yourself sucked into the story soon enough!

Who are you?


I am an enthusiast of ancient history, in particular the history of the Roman empire.

I have been obsessed with ancient history since at age 9 my parents made the mistake of letting me stay up late one night to watch the BBC production of "I, Claudius"! Since then, I've had an insatiable desire to learn more about Rome and roman history.

I've studied archaeology, antique studies and history at Stockholm university and consider myself well-read on the topic enough to guide beginnners, even though I am not a publishing academic. This site is my attempt to inspire that same desire to learn more about the period in you!
